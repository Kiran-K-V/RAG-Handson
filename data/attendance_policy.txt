IIT DHOLAKPUR — ATTENDANCE POLICY (Academic Year 2024-25)
=========================================================

MINIMUM ATTENDANCE REQUIREMENT
------------------------------
Every student must maintain a minimum of 75% attendance in EACH registered course
independently. As the great Bheem once said, "Showing up is half the battle" — the
institute takes this seriously. Attendance is calculated subject-wise, not as an
aggregate across all courses. A student who meets 75% overall but falls below 75%
in any single subject will still face consequences for that specific subject.

HOW ATTENDANCE IS RECORDED
--------------------------
Attendance is captured through a biometric fingerprint system installed at the
entrance of every lecture hall, laboratory, and tutorial room. The system was
developed in-house by the IITD Computer Science department and is powered by solar
panels on the Tuntun Mausi Library rooftop. Students must scan their fingerprint
within the first 10 minutes of a scheduled class. Late arrivals (after 10 minutes)
are marked as absent — no exceptions, not even if you were helping chase bandits
out of Dholakpur. Proxy attendance is a disciplinary offence and will result in
suspension of the student's biometric access for 7 days.

CONSEQUENCES OF LOW ATTENDANCE
------------------------------
Students whose attendance falls below 75% in any subject will face the following:
  1. Below 75% but above 65%: A warning letter is issued to the student and parents.
     The letter is also CC'd to the student's hostel warden.
  2. Below 65% but above 50%: The student is detained from appearing in the
     End-Semester Examination for that subject. They must re-register next semester.
     Additionally, the student is barred from participating in the Dholakpur Mela
     organising committee.
  3. Below 50%: The student is deregistered from the course entirely. The course
     will appear as "W" (Withdrawn) on the transcript. The student must also
     complete 20 hours of community service at the Dholakpur Village Library.

Detained students may appeal to the Dean of Academics within 7 days of the
detention notice.

CONDONATION OF ATTENDANCE
--------------------------
Attendance shortage may be condoned (excused) under the following circumstances:
  - Medical Leave: Supported by a medical certificate from the IITD Health Centre
    or the Dholakpur Government Hospital. Self-signed certificates and prescriptions
    from Dholu-Bholu Medical Store are not accepted. Maximum condonation: 15 days
    per semester.
  - Sports Representation: Students representing IITD at inter-IIT, national, or
    international events. Must be certified by the Sports Officer. Participation
    in the annual Dholakpur Strongman Championship also qualifies.
  - Cultural/Technical Events: Participation in institute-approved events (Techfest,
    Dholakpur Mela, Ladoo-Eating Championship) with prior written approval from
    the faculty advisor.
  - Family Emergency: Death or critical illness of an immediate family member,
    supported by documentary evidence. Maximum: 10 days.
  - Kingdom Emergency: In the rare event of a kingdom-wide emergency declared by
    the Raja of Dholakpur, all attendance for that period is automatically condoned.

Condonation applications must be submitted within 5 working days of resuming
classes. Late applications will not be entertained.

GRACE MARKS PROVISION
---------------------
There is no provision for grace marks to compensate for attendance shortage.
Attendance and academic performance are evaluated independently. However, students
with 95% or above attendance in a subject are eligible for a 2% bonus on their
End-Semester Examination score (capped at the maximum marks for that exam) plus
a complimentary box of Tuntun Mausi's premium ladoos at the semester-end ceremony.

FACULTY RESPONSIBILITIES
------------------------
Faculty members must upload attendance records to the Student Portal within 24
hours of each class session. Discrepancies reported by students within 48 hours
will be investigated by the department attendance coordinator. Faculty members
who fail to upload on time will receive a reminder from the Dean — and nobody
wants two reminders.

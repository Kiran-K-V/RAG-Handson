IIT DOLAKHPUR — ATTENDANCE POLICY (Academic Year 2024-25)
=========================================================

MINIMUM ATTENDANCE REQUIREMENT
------------------------------
Every student must maintain a minimum of 75% attendance in EACH registered course
independently. Attendance is calculated subject-wise, not as an aggregate across
all courses. A student who meets 75% overall but falls below 75% in any single
subject will still face consequences for that specific subject.

HOW ATTENDANCE IS RECORDED
--------------------------
Attendance is captured through a biometric fingerprint system installed at the
entrance of every lecture hall, laboratory, and tutorial room. Students must scan
their fingerprint within the first 10 minutes of a scheduled class. Late arrivals
(after 10 minutes) are marked as absent. Proxy attendance is a disciplinary offence
and will result in suspension of the student's biometric access for 7 days.

CONSEQUENCES OF LOW ATTENDANCE
------------------------------
Students whose attendance falls below 75% in any subject will face the following:
  1. Below 75% but above 65%: A warning letter is issued to the student and parents.
  2. Below 65% but above 50%: The student is detained from appearing in the
     End-Semester Examination for that subject. They must re-register next semester.
  3. Below 50%: The student is deregistered from the course entirely. The course
     will appear as "W" (Withdrawn) on the transcript.

Detained students may appeal to the Dean of Academics within 7 days of the
detention notice.

CONDONATION OF ATTENDANCE
--------------------------
Attendance shortage may be condoned (excused) under the following circumstances:
  - Medical Leave: Supported by a medical certificate from the IITD Health Centre
    or a government hospital. Self-signed certificates are not accepted. Maximum
    condonation: 15 days per semester.
  - Sports Representation: Students representing IITD at inter-IIT, national, or
    international events. Must be certified by the Sports Officer.
  - Cultural/Technical Events: Participation in institute-approved events (Techfest,
    Culrav) with prior written approval from the faculty advisor.
  - Family Emergency: Death or critical illness of an immediate family member,
    supported by documentary evidence. Maximum: 10 days.

Condonation applications must be submitted within 5 working days of resuming
classes. Late applications will not be entertained.

GRACE MARKS PROVISION
---------------------
There is no provision for grace marks to compensate for attendance shortage.
Attendance and academic performance are evaluated independently. However, students
with 95% or above attendance in a subject are eligible for a 2% bonus on their
End-Semester Examination score (capped at the maximum marks for that exam).

FACULTY RESPONSIBILITIES
------------------------
Faculty members must upload attendance records to the Student Portal within 24
hours of each class session. Discrepancies reported by students within 48 hours
will be investigated by the department attendance coordinator.

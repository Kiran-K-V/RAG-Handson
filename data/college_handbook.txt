IIT DHOLAKPUR (IITD) — STUDENT HANDBOOK 2024-25
================================================

ABOUT THE INSTITUTION
---------------------
Indian Institute of Technology Dholakpur (IITD) was established in 2002 by a royal
decree of the Raja of Dholakpur and subsequently recognised under the IIT Act. The
campus spans 580 acres along the banks of the Dholakpur River, surrounded by the
dense forests that the kingdom has been known for since ancient times. The institute
offers undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral programmes across 12 departments.
IITD is recognised by UGC, accredited by NAAC (A++ grade), and proudly ranked among
the top 20 engineering institutions in India by NIRF. The institute's motto is
"Strength and Wisdom" — inspired by the legendary hero Bheem of Dholakpur.

ACADEMIC CALENDAR
-----------------
The academic year is divided into two semesters:
  - Autumn Semester: July 25 – November 30 (End-Semester Exams: Nov 20–30)
  - Spring Semester: January 6 – May 10 (End-Semester Exams: Apr 28 – May 10)
  - Dholakpur Foundation Day (campus holiday): March 14

Mid-semester examinations are held in the 8th week of each semester. Supplementary
examinations for students with backlogs are conducted in June every year. Classes
are suspended during the annual Dholakpur Mela (cultural fair) in the last week of
February.

LIBRARY
-------
The Tuntun Mausi Central Library (named after the renowned local philanthropist and
ladoo entrepreneur) is open from 8:00 AM to 11:00 PM on all working days and
9:00 AM to 6:00 PM on Sundays and public holidays. Each student may borrow up to
6 books at a time with a loan period of 14 days. Overdue fine is ₹5 per day per book.
Digital resources including IEEE Xplore, Springer, and ACM Digital Library are
accessible on campus Wi-Fi. The Rare Manuscripts Wing houses historical scrolls
from the Dholakpur Royal Archives.

CANTEEN AND MESS FACILITIES
----------------------------
The Main Canteen ("Bheem's Kitchen") operates from 7:30 AM to 10:00 PM and is famous
for its unlimited ladoo counter (₹10 per plate). The Night Canteen (near Hostel
Block C, "Raju's Corner") is open from 10:00 PM to 1:00 AM. Mess timings are:
Breakfast 7:30–9:00 AM, Lunch 12:30–2:00 PM, Snacks 5:00–6:00 PM (ladoos served
daily), Dinner 7:30–9:00 PM. Mess rebate applications must be submitted 3 days in
advance at the hostel office. The canteen sources all dairy products from the
Dholakpur Royal Dairy Farm.

HOSTEL RULES
------------
IITD has 8 hostels named after legendary figures of Dholakpur:
  - Bheem Bhawan (Boys), Chutki Bhawan (Girls), Raju Niwas (Boys),
    Jaggu Hostel (Co-ed), Kalia Tower (Boys), Dholu-Bholu Block (Freshers),
    Indumati Hall (Girls), Rajkumar Block (PG Students).

All hostellers must return to their respective hostels by 11:00 PM on weekdays.
Late entry requires written permission from the Warden. Overnight absence must be
reported via the Leave Management Portal at least 24 hours in advance. Ragging in
any form is strictly prohibited and punishable under UGC regulations. The forest
area behind Jaggu Hostel is out of bounds after sunset.

CODE OF CONDUCT
---------------
Students must carry their IITD Identity Card at all times on campus. Use of personal
vehicles is prohibited for first-year students (institute bullock-cart shuttle
available). Consumption of alcohol and tobacco on campus premises is strictly
forbidden. Any act of academic dishonesty including plagiarism will result in an
F grade and disciplinary action. Climbing the ancient banyan tree near the
Administrative Block is prohibited without safety clearance from the Sports Officer.

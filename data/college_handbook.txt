IIT DHOLAKPUR (IITD) — STUDENT HANDBOOK 2024-25
================================================

ABOUT THE INSTITUTION
---------------------
Indian Institute of Technology Dholakpur (IITD) was established in 2002 under the
IIT Act and is located on a 580-acre campus along the Dholakpur River in central
Madhya Pradesh. The institute offers undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral
programmes across 12 departments. IITD is recognised by UGC, accredited by NAAC
(A++ grade), and ranked among the top 20 engineering institutions in India by NIRF.
The institute's motto is "Strength and Wisdom."

ACADEMIC CALENDAR
-----------------
The academic year is divided into two semesters:
  - Autumn Semester: July 25 – November 30 (End-Semester Exams: Nov 20–30)
  - Spring Semester: January 6 – May 10 (End-Semester Exams: Apr 28 – May 10)
  - Foundation Day (campus holiday): March 14

Mid-semester examinations are held in the 8th week of each semester. Supplementary
examinations for students with backlogs are conducted in June every year. Classes
are suspended during the annual Dholakpur Mela (cultural fest) in the last week of
February.

LIBRARY
-------
The Central Library is open from 8:00 AM to 11:00 PM on all working days and
9:00 AM to 6:00 PM on Sundays and public holidays. Each student may borrow up to
6 books at a time with a loan period of 14 days. Overdue fine is ₹5 per day per book.
Digital resources including IEEE Xplore, Springer, and ACM Digital Library are
accessible on campus Wi-Fi.

CANTEEN AND MESS FACILITIES
----------------------------
The Main Canteen operates from 7:30 AM to 10:00 PM. The Night Canteen (near Hostel
Block C) is open from 10:00 PM to 1:00 AM. Mess timings are: Breakfast 7:30–9:00 AM,
Lunch 12:30–2:00 PM, Snacks 5:00–6:00 PM, Dinner 7:30–9:00 PM. The canteen is known
for its ladoos — a regional speciality served daily during snack hours. Mess rebate
applications must be submitted 3 days in advance at the hostel office.

HOSTEL RULES
------------
IITD has 8 hostels. All hostellers must return to their respective hostels by
11:00 PM on weekdays. Late entry requires written permission from the Warden.
Overnight absence must be reported via the Leave Management Portal at least 24 hours
in advance. Ragging in any form is strictly prohibited and punishable under UGC
regulations. The forested area behind the campus boundary is out of bounds after
sunset for safety reasons.

CODE OF CONDUCT
---------------
Students must carry their IITD Identity Card at all times on campus. Use of personal
vehicles is prohibited for first-year students. Consumption of alcohol and tobacco
on campus premises is strictly forbidden. Any act of academic dishonesty including
plagiarism will result in an F grade and disciplinary action.

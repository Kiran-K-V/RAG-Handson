IIT DHOLAKPUR — PLACEMENT GUIDELINES 2024-25
=============================================

ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA
--------------------
To be eligible for campus placements, a student must satisfy ALL of the following:
  1. Minimum CGPA of 6.0 (on a 10-point scale) at the time of registration.
  2. No active backlog (failed subject) at the time of the placement drive.
     Students with cleared backlogs are eligible.
  3. Minimum 75% attendance in the current semester.
  4. No pending disciplinary action from the Dean of Students office.

Students who have accepted a Pre-Placement Offer (PPO) from an internship company
are not eligible to sit for other companies unless the PPO is formally declined in
writing before August 15.

REGISTRATION PROCESS
--------------------
  - Registration opens: August 1 (via the Placement Portal at placement.iitdholakpur.ac.in)
  - Registration deadline: August 20
  - Document verification: August 21–25
  - Placement season begins: September 1

Students must upload the following during registration: updated resume (max 1 page),
passport-size photograph, consolidated marksheet, and links to coding profiles
(LeetCode, GitHub, or equivalent). Resume must be formatted using the official IITD
template available on the portal.

COMPANY TIERS
-------------
Companies are classified into three tiers based on CTC (Cost to Company):
  - Mass Recruiters: CTC up to ₹8 LPA. Students may accept one Mass Recruiter offer
    and still appear for higher tiers.
  - Dream: CTC between ₹8 LPA and ₹25 LPA. Once a Dream offer is accepted, the
    student is removed from the Dream pool but may still sit for Super Dream.
  - Super Dream: CTC above ₹25 LPA. Once a Super Dream offer is accepted, the
    student is removed from all placement pools.

A student may hold at most one offer at any time. If a higher-tier offer is received,
the lower-tier offer is automatically released.

PLACEMENT TIMELINE
------------------
  - September 1–15: Pre-Placement Talks (PPTs) by registered companies.
  - September 16–30: Online assessments and coding tests.
  - October 1 onwards: Interview rounds (Day 0, Day 1, Day 2, etc.)
  - Day 0 (October 1): Super Dream companies only.
  - Day 1 (October 2): Dream companies.
  - Day 2 onwards: Mass Recruiters and remaining companies.

WHAT STUDENTS MUST PREPARE
---------------------------
  - Resume: Single page, peer-reviewed, approved by the Placement Cell.
  - Coding Profiles: Active profiles on LeetCode (minimum 150 problems solved),
    GitHub with at least 3 public repositories.
  - Core Subjects: Data Structures, Algorithms, OS, DBMS, Computer Networks.
  - Aptitude: Quantitative aptitude, logical reasoning, verbal ability.
  - Communication: Group discussion and HR interview preparation. The Placement
    Cell conducts free mock GD sessions every Saturday.

OFF-CAMPUS PLACEMENTS
---------------------
Students may pursue off-campus placements at any time. However, they must inform
the Placement Cell before accepting an off-campus offer. Off-campus offers count
towards tier restrictions — a student who accepts an off-campus Dream offer cannot
sit for on-campus Dream companies. Off-campus companies must be verified by the
Placement Cell to ensure legitimacy.

PRE-PLACEMENT TALKS AND TEST FORMAT
------------------------------------
Companies conduct a 45-minute PPT before their recruitment process. Attendance at
the PPT is mandatory for students who wish to appear for that company's selection
process. Tests are typically conducted on HackerEarth or HackerRank platforms and
last 60–90 minutes.

INTERNSHIP GUIDELINES
---------------------
All pre-final year students are required to complete a mandatory summer internship
(minimum 8 weeks) between May and July. The Placement Cell facilitates internship
drives starting in January. Students may also pursue self-arranged internships,
subject to approval by the department internship coordinator. Internship reports
must be submitted within 2 weeks of completion and carry 4 academic credits.
Students with outstanding internship performance may receive a Pre-Placement Offer
(PPO) from the company.

DRESS CODE FOR PLACEMENT EVENTS
--------------------------------
Students appearing for PPTs, tests, and interviews must adhere to formal dress code:
  - Men: Formal shirt, trousers, closed shoes. No jeans or sneakers.
  - Women: Formal attire (kurta/shirt with trousers, salwar suit, or saree). No casual wear.
  - ID card must be worn visibly during all placement events.

ANTI-BLACKLISTING POLICY
--------------------------
Students who accept an offer and later renege (back out) will be blacklisted from
the placement process for the remainder of the season. The blacklisting also affects
the company's willingness to recruit from IITD in future years. Students facing
genuine hardship may appeal to the Placement Committee within 48 hours.
